Fun Facts

BIRTH DATE: April 1, 1940

PLACE OF BIRTH: Nyeri, Kenya

DEATH DATE: September 25, 2011

EDUCATION: Mount St. Scholastica College, University of Pittsburg, University of Nairobi.

FAMOUS QUOTE: "We cannot tire or give up. We owe it to the present and future generations of all species to rise up and walk!"

Biography

     Wangari Maathai was born in Nyeri, Kenya in 1940. She later moved to the United States of America to persue her dream of earning her doctrate degree. She attended Mount St. Scholastica College in 1964 where she obtained a degree in Biological Sciences. After that, she attended the University of Pittsburg where she earned a Master of Science degree. She later moved to Germany to attend the University of Nairobi where she obtained a Ph.D. and she also taught veterinary anatomy. A few years later, she became the chair of the department for Veterinary Anatomy and an associate professor. Wangari became the first person in Africa to become an associate professor and to obtain her doctrate-degree. In 1976, Wangari established the organization "The Green Belt Movement" to help raise awareness about environmental conservation by planting trees everywhere.
